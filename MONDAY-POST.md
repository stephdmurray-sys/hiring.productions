## The first hour after you post — the part that compounds reach

LinkedIn's algorithm weighs first-hour engagement heavily. Whatever happens in the first 60 minutes determines how far your post reaches over the next 5 days.

**Minute 0 — post**

**Minute 5 — leave the first substantive comment yourself.**

Pick the comment that adds value AND opens a thread. Something like:

> If you want the specific 3 moves to climb out of the founder-title trap, the simulator outputs them as part of the result. The most common one I've seen so far: candidates keeping "Founder" but adding a second descriptor like "ex-VP People @ [last full-time company]" in the headline. Recruiter queries hit on the second title and the candidate stays accurate about their current work.

This comment does three things: shows you're active on your own post (algorithm signal), gives the early scrollers a second value-add, and seeds the comment thread with substance so others reply to it.

**Minute 10–60 — engage with every comment.**

For each comment that comes in:
- Respond with a real reply (not "Thanks!" — actually engage with what they said)
- Ask them a question if natural ("What role are you currently sourced for?" "What's the headline you're testing?")
- This converts each comment into a 2-3 comment thread which signals depth to the algorithm

**Hour 2–24 — light engagement maintenance.**

- Reply to every comment within 6 hours of posting
- DM (not reply) anyone who says "I ran it and got #X" — those are your conversion conversations. Offer to look at their result if they want.

## What you DON'T need to do

- Don't write a 5-paragraph LinkedIn essay. The post above is intentionally tight.
- Don't tag anyone in the post. Tags are noise; they don't help reach.
- Don't add a "thoughts?" or "agree/disagree?" prompt. Those read as engagement-bait now.
- Don't post the same content to the company page on the same day. Wait at least 3 days, then repost there with a different framing.
- Don't worry about response volume in the first 30 minutes. LinkedIn's algorithm gives every post a "probationary" reach of about 100 impressions; engagement during that window determines whether it extends.

---

## If the post bombs

If you have under 1,000 impressions and under 5 comments after 24 hours, the post didn't work. Three possible reasons:
1. **Time of day was off.** Try Tuesday at the same time next week with a different post from the queue.
2. **Audience-format mismatch.** Your recent audience growth may have shifted away from hiring/TA content. We'd test a different hook.
3. **LinkedIn shadow-throttling.** Posting frequency, unusual link patterns, or a low recent-engagement-rate can cause this. We'd diagnose.

Don't repost the same content. Don't delete and re-post (LinkedIn penalizes that). Move to Week 2 next Monday and we'll iterate.
