# Hiring-team tool backlog (2026)

Research-validated punch list of AI tools to build for hiring teams.
Generated from a deep web search across HR-Brew, Inc., BambooHR, Jackson
Lewis, AIHR, Recruiterflow, and Reddit secondary-source threads.

The 3 tools currently in build (JD-Through-Candidate-Eyes, AI Candidate
Detector, Boolean Search Builder) sit alongside this list — these are
what comes after.

## Top 10, ranked by urgency × buildability

1. **Applicant Triage Heatmap** — paste JD + resume batch, get ranked
   shortlist with explainable scoring and AI-vs-human tags. Solves the
   "1,000-applicant threshold" that's now standard for any decent
   posting. Pairs with the AI Candidate Detector as the volume layer.

2. **Pay Range Compliance Generator** — role + work locations →
   defensible posted range + jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction compliance
   notes + copy-paste disclosure language. 17 states + DC require this
   in 2026; remote-role rules are the trap small teams miss. Penalties
   are per-posting per-day.

3. **Scorecard Builder from JD** — ingest a JD, output 6-10 competencies
   with behavioral anchors and a 4-point rubric. Structured scorecards
   raise predictive validity from .20 to .51 but nobody builds them
   per-role. Exportable to Greenhouse / Ashby / Notion.

4. **Intake Brief Generator** — short questionnaire for the hiring
   manager produces a one-page role brief (must-haves, nice-to-haves,
   calibration examples, sourcing channels, kill criteria). Misalignment
   between recruiter and hiring manager is the #1 driver of bad hires
   at small companies.

5. **Bulk Rejection Personalizer** — drop in candidate notes + stage →
   short, specific, on-brand rejection emails by stage. Auto-rejected
   gets one treatment, final-round gets another. Addresses the 53% of
   candidates who get ghosted.

6. **Offer Pitch Builder** — role + comp + candidate priorities →
   written offer pitch emphasizing scope / autonomy / equity tradeoffs
   vs. named competitors, plus a counter-offer playbook. Small companies
   lose 10-14-day-old top candidates to faster, richer competitors.

7. **Reference Check Kit** — role-specific question set (5 structured +
   3 behavioral + 2 red-flag probes) + backchannel intro email template
   + scoring rubric. References are now rubber-stamps; backchannel is
   standard but the script gets reinvented every time.

8. **Offer Letter Generator** — state-aware template (CA/NY/CO/WA quirks
   baked in) with equity language, contingencies, and a clean "rescind
   safely" mode. Solo founders writing offer letters in Google Docs
   miss at-will, classification, equity, or state-specific clauses.

9. **Sourcing Channel Planner** — role + budget → ranked channel mix
   (referral program template, niche boards, GitHub/Stack queries,
   Slack/Discord communities) with weekly time estimates. LinkedIn
   Recruiter is $10K+/seat; small teams need a budgetless playbook.

10. **AI Hiring Vendor Compliance Scanner** — vendor name or audit
    summary → plain-English risk readout (impact ratio, four-fifths
    rule pass/fail, what to publicly post). NYC Local Law 144
    enforcement quiet period ends 2026; CA/NJ/IL legislation in
    flight; EEOC says "the AI made me do it" is not a defense.

## Bench (worth building eventually)

- **No-show predictor + re-engagement nudges** (22% offer-accept
  no-show rate; 41% of orgs report candidate ghosting)
- **RTO policy stress-test** — predict how a 3-day / 4-day / 5-day
  RTO policy will shrink your applicant pool for a given role / geo

## 2026 macro context worth tracking

- **The "1,000-applicant threshold"** — single tech/marketing/remote
  roles routinely hit it. Dominant 2026 narrative on r/recruiting and
  HR-Brew.
- **AI-resume inflation** — 31% of candidates use AI to write resumes;
  recruiters can't tell signal from polish.
- **Bias audit enforcement going live** — NYC LL144 quiet period ends
  2026; CA/NJ/IL legislation in flight.
- **AI-interview backlash** — nearly 4 in 10 candidates bail when an
  AI interviewer is required. Brand risk for the employer.
- **Pay transparency expansion** — DE law effective Sept 2027;
  remote-role coverage rules are the trap most small employers miss.
- **RTO as hiring filter** — full-RTO mandates shrink applicant pools
  50-70%. Remote roles draw 60% of LinkedIn applications despite being
  20% of postings.
- **Speed-to-offer as the new differentiator** — top candidates off
  the market in 10-14 days. Small companies' structural advantage if
  they tool up.
- **Candidate-side ghosting flipping** — 41% of employers now report
  being ghosted, not just an applicant complaint.
